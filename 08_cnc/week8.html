Week 8: CNC Milling

This week was about manufacturing processes using the CNC mill and molding + casting techniques. The task was to first mill something, then post-process it with one of the techniques we learned about.

CNC Mill

Since I ended up choosing my MBTA tracker idea for my final project, I wanted to honor my other project idea and CNC a catan tile. Back in Week 6, we prepared our CAD files for milling. I used Fusion 360 to create a 3D model of a wheat tile, using published known dimensions. My original design ended up not having enough spacing between some lines, so I had to reduce the amount of wheat on my design and make things larger. The final design is shown above.

The file needed for the ShopBot was a .dxf file, so I exported the design as a 2D drawing and saved it as a .dxf. When Bobby was helping me using the machine, we found out there were a bunch of open vectors, which was a result of sketch geometries being duplicated. I ended up having to go back into Fusion, do some careful projecting, and then re-export the file. The result ended up being pretty clean and only took about 15 minutes to mill.

Post-Processing!

For post-processing, I chose to vacuum form the tile. I used the small Micro-Mark vacuum former, which just barely fit the tile. Snapped a piece of plastic on that bad boy, let it heat up, and then hit the vacuum. The resulting mold was decent, but due to the tile's size and sharpness, the plastic didn't fully form around the edges. It was also a pain to remove from the mold, and some wood ended up getting stuck in the plastic as you can see below.

I then poured some plaster into the mold and let it set. It look pretty decent, while in the mold. Then, I tried to remove it. It started to crack and break, and eventually I gave up keeping it together. The result was a bunch of pieces of plaster that I didn't want to play puzzle with to glue back together. If I have some more time, I'll look into redoing the mold and casting.
