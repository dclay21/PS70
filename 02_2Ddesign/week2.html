Week 2: 2D Design and Cutting

This week was an intro to laser cutting and a bit of fusion. I've laser cut a couple times before, but this was my first time crafting something without strict guidance.

Main Assignment: Cardboard Box

The major assignment was creating a box to hold our materials for the rest of the semester, and in thinking about how to stay on my game theme, I chose to make my box a gamecube! The gamecube is just a little bit before my time, but I did play Mario Kart DoubleDash so I think I'm valid.

Design

Because I like seeing things fully assembled, I opted to 3d model the box by each side in Fusion, and then transform that into a 2D sketch I could laser cut. So, after some extrusions, some patterning, and a few rotational joints later, I had my unfolded box.

Five big booms to Kassia for showing us how the Project tool in Fusion works. After using it during lab to model our useless box, I decided that's how I'd laser cut my box. I projected all of the components onto one of the origin planes and then continued to sketch up some extra decorations, leading to this nice final sketch below. All that's left is to cut and assemble.

Laser Cutting and Assembly

I had a few worries initially about cutting the sides, since I had a lot of small squares I wanted to fully cut out. And surely enough, those worries came to fruition. A small fire did start (look towards the top of the right pic), but I put it out quickly and decided I needed a new plan. So, instead, I decided to score the squares as a little design. I probably could've then cut them out with an x-acto knife, but I didn't have the activation energy. Another approach suggested was to do the cut on lower power and do a couple of passes, but honestly I was getting close to the deadline. If I have some spare time, maybe I'll do that and replace the sides. Otherwise, cutting went pretty smoothly, kerf thankfully wasn't a problem, and the folding hinges I used to make the handle and foldable top worked suprisingly well.

I'm honestly pretty satisfied with the result! I think it would be pretty cool to add a cardboard controller along side it, but yeah, time and all that.
