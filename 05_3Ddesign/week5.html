Week 5: 3D Design and Printing

And the moment we've all been waiting for, 3D printing! Having this under my belt definitely helped with progress towards the final project, and even assignments in other classes.

Main Assignment: Design and Print Something

So I actually ended up using this assignment to work on something useful for another one of my classes. For MIT's 6.900, we have a final project where we need to create a set of small, remote, weather monitoring systems to be placed across campus and accurately measure temperature and humidity. Last year's class used a solid box to house everything, and they saw errors in measurements around midday, when temperatures were at their highest. We decided it would be a good idea to iterate over the design, and allow for airflow so the box doesn't hold so much heat.

This led me to design a Stevenson shield box with vents to house the sensors. I definitely think I had the right idea, but I put myself into a lot of struggle with how I chose to do supports. In my naivety, I left supports everywhere, and since I had hollowed out some of the walls of the box with my vent creation, those supports were almost impossible to remove. We ended up changing our design anyways to make a smaller cone-like radiation shield for the sensors. I also 3d printed a basic, solid box to mimic the previous year's design, and used that for testing the effectiveness of our new shield (airflow was infact good).

Photogrammetry/LIDAR

I used the Polycam app to create a 3D model of the first interesting looking object I could find in lab, and that was a small box of corn starch. To do the scan, I setup the corn starch in the PS70 filming booth, which has good lighting that prevents unwanted shadows. I then used the auto function in the app on my phone to continuously take pictures of the object and rotated around it. After only a minute, I had come up with the model below. I've provided a picture of the setup for reference too. I was wary about going all the way around the object because I might just not even capture it my lens, so the backside isn't rendered very well. Otherwise, it came out pretty well, even capturing the text.
