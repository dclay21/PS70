Final Project

The main assignment for this week is to start this website and come up with some project ideas. So here's two of my current ideas.

Idea 1: Train Tracker Decoration

Going from a state where I've always needed a car to cities with public transporation has made me a big subway advocate. This first project idea is replicating the train tracker from the gif, while placing inside a 3d modeled or laser cut city map to make it look more aesthetic as a wall decoration. The train tracker would pull subway data live from the MBTA api and display it on the map via LEDs. I'm trying to envision a different way of indicating direction that's not just two separate tracks for each line, but it's still a work in progress.

Idea 2: Custom Catan Board (w/electronics)

So I played Catan for the first time during my sophomore year of college, and it was wraps from there. If someone proposes a game in my vicinity, I will play without hesitation. Moving on, I thought it would be pretty cool to make my own custom board and incorporate some electronics. I've seen examples of boards where people laser cut or 3d print their own pieces, and I'd also like to do that, making my own designs for the different resources tiles, the water borders, game pieces, etc. I've seen some boards that are electronic as well, having an automatic dice roller programmed by a microcontroller, which then lights up the correct tiles on the board that correspond to the roll so people collect their resources. However, those boards aren't usually modular and can't be packed up very easily. My goal is to make a board that can do both of those things. I don't want to do virtual dice, since I feel the whole rolling dice thing really adds to the board game aspect, so I want to make a dice box or cage to roll the dice in. From there, a camera + microcontroller processes the roll, either by some CV techniques or color/pattern recognition, and then it's time for the lighting. The difficulty here is trying to keep the setup modular. I could mimic how nanoleaf tiles connect (see below) for circuitry, or I could look for a different solution.

One of my other alternatives is to have the lights be on the number tiles themselves (the ones you can place on any tile and signal resource collection for dice), and do something where the microcontroller broadcasts a frequency unique to each number, and some circuit there will pick up the signal and switch the light on if it's the right number. I'm not sure how feasible that is, but it's an idea.
